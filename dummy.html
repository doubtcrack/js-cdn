Bachelors In Medical Laboratory Technology (BMLT)

Bachelors in Medical Laboratory Technology (BMLT), also known as clinical laboratory Technology is a 3-year undergraduate program that can be pursued after completion of Class 12th.

 

It is a career-oriented course and is a popular one in paramedical science. Global Medical Laboratories Market size was valued at $78Bn by 2019 and is anticipated to grow at a CAGR of 5.4% during the forecast period 2020-2025.

It is an allied health profession which is concerned with the diagnosis and prevention of disease through the use of clinical laboratory investigations and findings.

The efficient and balanced training program in laboratory medicine, allows students to learn, detect and diagnose diseases.

The study also includes the know-how of collecting information required, sampling, testing, reporting and documentation of the investigations.

DURATION 4 Years

ELIGIBILITY

Sr. Secondary (10+2) with Biology or Life Sciences as one of the subjects with minimum 50% marks.

An entrance exam followed by personal interview with ICRI.

CAREER
OPPORTUNITY

The course aims to provide the candidate with adequate knowledge and skills to carry out routine laboratory diagnostic procedures and some sophisticated laboratory diagnostic procedures.

They would have sufficient theoretical knowledge related to the principles of the laboratory exercises and also to deal with new advances in instrumentation, methodology or standardization, Management of Laboratories, Standard Protocols and use of technology as relates to Laboratory Investigations. Medical laboratory technologists do routine laboratory testing under stipulated conditions.

Technologists may prepare specimens and operate machines that automatically analyse samples. The average salary observed by candidates pursuing Bachelor in Medical Laboratory Technology (BMLT) is INR 2 lacs to 12 lacs per annum depending upon the candidature.
